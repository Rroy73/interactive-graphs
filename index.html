Graph 1

This graph highlights India's inflation trajectory and RBI's stable rates from 2016–2020, contrasting with the U.S. Fed's aggressive hikes post-2016 following Trump’s pro-growth fiscal policies. While the U.S. raised rates to counter inflationary pressures from tax cuts and spending, the RBI kept rates steady, owing to it mainly being due to cost-pull factors and focus on growth.

Graph 2

This graph highlights the steady increase in the CPI-U (inflation) from 2005 to 2020, reflecting rising prices. Post-2016, after Donald Trump's election, Trump's promises of tax cuts and fiscal ctimulus coulped with economic growth led to inflationary pressures. The Federal Reserve responded by gradually increasing the Fed Funds Rate from near zero, peaking around 2018, to control inflation.

Graph 3

This graph shows India's exports to the U.S., which grew significantly during Trump's presidency (2016–2020), driven by robust U.S. demand and India's competitiveness. Trade policies under Trump, including tariffs on China, likely diverted some U.S. demand toward India.
